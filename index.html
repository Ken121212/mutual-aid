SOCIAL DISTANCING

COVID-19 spreads mainly among people who are in close contact (within about 6 feet) for a prolonged period. Spread happens when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, and droplets from their mouth or nose are launched into the air and land in the mouths or noses of people nearby. The droplets can also be inhaled into the lungs. Recent studies indicate that people who are infected but do not have symptoms likely also play a role in the spread of COVID-19.


WEARING MASKS

Wearing masks is an effective way to prevent virus spreading through cough, sneezes, talks, or floating droplets in the air. COVID-19 could spread even the infected is asymptomatic, no one could know if they themselves are infected, so it is mutual beneficial for you and others to wear masks. It is less likely to be infected if you wear N95, KN95, or 3 layer face masks.


WASHING HANDS

Wash you hands immediately after you went back home. The COVID-19 virus could survive 2 hours in aerosol, as long as it falls on the surface of object, it could survive up to 24 hours. Please avoid touching things outside your house and do not touch your eyes, nose, mouth and face since it could infect you through mucosal infection


STRENGTHEN IMMUNE SYSTEM

Currently, there is no vaccine and no targeted medicine proven effective for this virus. So the sometthing you could do to prevent this virus is to eat more high protein food, like fish, beef, eggs and milk. Do some indoor exercises and also sleep and rest enough everyday.


SELF ISOLATION

If you found yourself contacted someone who is infected COVID-19, ask for a test kit and isolate yourself for 14 days. It is dangerous to live with your family members while you are infected. This might increase the risk of spreading this virus to your family members.
